Fred the Baker was born in 1871 in New York City. He didn't have many friends in school, and he was bullied quite often because he would always have icing on his shirt and sprinkles in his hair. On his way to school everyday, he would pass by a bakery. He planned to put in his application once he turned 14. But as a child, he would just volunteer to clean the floors and take out the trash. He wanted to learn how to make donuts so badly, but he knew he needed to wait. Finally, on Fred's 14th birthday, he woke up at 7:00 am to hand in his application, and he was hired immediately. He learned how to make the best donuts in town, and even came up with a few of his own recipes. His signature donut was a vanilla cake donut with pink icing and colorful sprinkles. "Donut Maker" was inspired by Fred and his famous donut. Thank you Fred for your service.
